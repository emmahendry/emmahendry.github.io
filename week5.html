    Explanation of cyber communities and incel media

    The Serious Issue with Incels

    Cyber communities are societies that exist for the purpose of sharing interests, socialising, education, or work-based contexts virtually. Their existence allows for people to connect all over the globe at any point in time. Cyber communities are a great innovation for people to find like-minded individuals from shared interests in bands on social media platforms to coding communities. The major takeaway is that cyber communities are a positive creation, allowing for the development of relationships between people who may or may not have ever interacted in real life. Despite this, there is the negative influences which cyber communities cultivate with bringing people together from around the world, through the ability to hide behind a screen. There is a tendency for insalubrious communities to form, such as “Incels”. An “Incel`’ is a word used to describe “a member of an online subculture of people who define themselves as incapable of attaining a romantic-sexual partner, even though they want one”. These communities tend to encourage misogynistic views towards women and obtain validation from other members for their detrimental opinions. A great example of someone who pushes this mindset is Andrew Tate.

    Andrew Tate

    Andrew Tate, the 36-year-old former world champion kickboxer turned “self-proclaimed misogynist” facing charges earlier this year when on trial for “rape, human trafficking and forming an organised crime group to sexually exploit women”. Tate found a new audience online through social media platforms and podcasts where he promoted his views on women which ultimately got him banned from YouTube, Facebook, Instagram and TikTok. He has also explicitly stated his hatred for women in interviews along with proudly stating he is sexist and anyone who isn’t is lying to themselves. Ultimately a person who has gained such a large platform like Andrew Tate, across his social media accounts, it is apparent the ideology behind his followers is similar to his own. Obviously, this is not a constructive influence on the younger members of today’s society and furthermore lends itself to the expanding of the “incel” community.
